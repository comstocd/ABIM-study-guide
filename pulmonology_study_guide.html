8. Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis High-Yield

Definition

  • Chronic, progressive fibrosing ILD of unknown cause
  • UIP pattern on HRCT or biopsy
  • Exclusion of known causes

Risk Factors

  • Age >60 (rare <50)
  • Male sex
  • Smoking history
  • GERD

Diagnosis

  • HRCT UIP pattern: Diagnostic if definite UIP + no identifiable cause
  • UIP features: Basal/peripheral predominance, honeycombing, traction bronchiectasis, reticular abnormality, absence of inconsistent features
  • Surgical lung biopsy: If HRCT indeterminate and would change management
Definite UIP on HRCT in appropriate clinical context = IPF diagnosis without biopsy. "Probable UIP" may also suffice with multidisciplinary discussion.

Treatment

DrugMechanismBenefit
PirfenidoneAntifibrotic (TGF-Œ≤ inhibition)Slows FVC decline
NintedanibTyrosine kinase inhibitorSlows FVC decline
  • Lung transplantation: Only curative option
  • Supplemental O2: For hypoxemia
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation
DO NOT use steroids or immunosuppressants in IPF ‚Äî PANTHER trial showed harm (prednisone + azathioprine + NAC increased mortality).

Prognosis

  • Median survival 3-5 years from diagnosis
  • Acute exacerbations carry high mortality (>50%)

23. ABG Interpretation High-Yield

Systematic Approach

  1. Acidemia or alkalemia? pH <7.35 = acidemia; pH >7.45 = alkalemia
  2. Primary disorder? Check PaCO2 (respiratory) and HCO3 (metabolic)
  3. Compensation appropriate? Calculate expected compensation
  4. If metabolic acidosis: Calculate anion gap
  5. If elevated AG: Calculate delta-delta ratio

Compensation Formulas


Metabolic acidosis: Expected PaCO2 = 1.5 √ó HCO3 + 8 (¬±2) [Winter's]
Metabolic alkalosis: Expected PaCO2 = 0.7 √ó HCO3 + 21 (¬±2)
Respiratory acidosis (acute): HCO3 ‚Üë 1 per 10 mmHg ‚Üë PaCO2
Respiratory acidosis (chronic): HCO3 ‚Üë 3.5 per 10 mmHg ‚Üë PaCO2
Respiratory alkalosis (acute): HCO3 ‚Üì 2 per 10 mmHg ‚Üì PaCO2
Respiratory alkalosis (chronic): HCO3 ‚Üì 5 per 10 mmHg ‚Üì PaCO2

Anion Gap


AG = Na - (Cl + HCO3)
Normal = 8-12 mEq/L

Delta-Delta Ratio


ŒîAG / ŒîHCO3 = (AG - 12) / (24 - HCO3)
<1 = concurrent non-AG metabolic acidosis
1-2 = pure AG metabolic acidosis
>2 = concurrent metabolic alkalosis
