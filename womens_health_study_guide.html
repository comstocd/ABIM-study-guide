Genitourinary Syndrome of Menopause (GSM)

Treatment

  • If inadequate: Low-dose vaginal estrogen (cream, tablet, ring)
  • Vaginal estrogen is safe even with history of breast cancer (minimal systemic absorption)
  • Ospemifene (oral SERM) ‚Äî alternative if vaginal application not tolerated

Ovarian Cancer Screening

‚ùå NOT recommended for average-risk women:
  • Neither CA-125 nor transvaginal US improves mortality
  • High false-positive rate leads to unnecessary surgery
  • PLCO trial: No benefit, harm from unnecessary procedures

Endometrial Cancer

  • No routine screening for average-risk women
  • Evaluate abnormal uterine bleeding promptly (especially postmenopausal)
  • Lynch syndrome: Offer annual endometrial biopsy starting age 30-35

  2. Hyperandrogenism (clinical: hirsutism, acne, alopecia; OR biochemical: ‚Üëtestosterone)

  • Prolactin (hyperprolactinemia)

  • Infertility ‚Äî anovulation
  • Nonalcoholic fatty liver disease

  • Exercise
  • Dietary modification

üíé High-Yield Board Pearls

‚ñº
Contraception:
  1. Copper IUD is the most effective emergency contraception
  2. Migraine WITH AURA = absolute contraindication to estrogen-containing contraceptives
  3. IUDs are safe in nulliparous women and immediately postpartum
  4. Only rifampin significantly reduces OCP efficacy (not other antibiotics)
  5. Depo-Provera causes reversible bone loss ‚Äî limit to 2 years if possible
Pregnancy:
  1. ACE-I and ARBs are teratogenic ‚Äî contraindicated in all trimesters
  2. HELLP = Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets ‚Äî variant of severe preeclampsia
  3. Magnesium sulfate is used for seizure prophylaxis in preeclampsia (NOT for BP control)
  4. LMWH is the anticoagulant of choice in pregnancy (warfarin is teratogenic)
  5. Gestational diabetes ‚Üí 50% lifetime risk of type 2 DM
  6. Left leg DVT is more common in pregnancy (uterine compression of left iliac vein)
Menopause:
  1. HRT is most beneficial when started <60 yo or <10 years post-menopause ("timing hypothesis")
  2. Women with intact uterus need estrogen + progestogen (endometrial protection)
  3. Low-dose vaginal estrogen is safe even with history of breast cancer
  4. Paroxetine 7.5mg is the only non-hormonal FDA-approved treatment for hot flashes
Screening:
  1. Cervical cancer screening starts at 21 (regardless of sexual activity)
  2. HPV primary testing is now preferred for ages 30-65
  3. Ovarian cancer screening is NOT recommended (even CA-125 + TVUS)
  4. BRCA carriers: Consider risk-reducing salpingo-oophorectomy by 35-45
  5. USPSTF 2024: Mammography for all women 40-74 every 2 years
PCOS:
  1. Rotterdam criteria: 2 of 3 (oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries)
  2. COCs are first-line for menstrual regulation AND hirsutism
  3. Metformin is for metabolic features, NOT first-line for menstrual irregularity
  4. Letrozole > clomiphene for ovulation induction (infertility)
  5. Screen for metabolic syndrome, OSA, and depression
Osteoporosis:
  1. Screen all women ‚â•65 with DXA; younger if high-risk
  2. Bisphosphonate drug holiday after 5 years (3 years for IV)
  3. Denosumab: Rebound vertebral fractures if stopped ‚Äî must transition to another agent
  4. Raloxifene reduces vertebral (not hip) fractures AND reduces breast cancer risk
